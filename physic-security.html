Physic security

Introduction to physical security

  • Physical security is the protection of personnel, hardware, software, networks and data from physical actions and events that could cause serious loss or damage to an enterprise, agency or institution.
  • Physical security has three important components: access control, surveillance and testing.
  • Data center: where central storage and processing data, and should be strictly protected from termites danger.
  • Need to identify all hazards and plan for manage them.

Physical hazards

  • Fire and explosion
  • Temperature and humidity
  • Natural disasters: floods, storms, lightning, earthquakes
  • Collapse
  • Chemical
  • Power failure
  • Lost communication signal
  • Damaged device
  • The destructive element: internal staff, thieves

Physical safety control

  • Administrative control: use policies/ laws to define how the information system operates properly, safely, confidentially.
  • Physical access control: use access control tools between the outside environment and information systems such as barriers, door locks and guards…
  • Technical control: use software, hardware to help protect the assets of the company/ organization.

Human management

  • Training to avoid operational risks.
  • Avoid attackers from outside.
  • Responsible for managing personal information and job security.
