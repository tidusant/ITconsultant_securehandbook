Security of the devices (computers, phones, smart cards, usb stics…)

Problems:

  • Reveal personal information.
  • Stolen important data.
  • Stolen money from bank account through internet banking in mobile.
  • Store valuable data and information.

Solutions:

  • Use strong, unique passwords for Wi-Fi networks and device accounts.
  • Unsure public or free wifi is protected.
  • Utilize VPN.
  • Disable features you may not need.
  • Encrypt your device.
  • Install an antivirus application.
  • Set up a guest network.
  • Audit the IoT devices already on your home network.
  • Change default usernames and passwords.
  • Update to the lasted software.
  • Log out everytime shut down device.
  • Use only trusted stores and avoid public Wi-Fi networks.

Conclusion:

We’ve described almost all devices that will increase security in your network. Some of them, such as firewalls and antivirus software, are must-have network security devices; others are nice to have. Before implementing any new security device, always perform an IT security risk assessment; it will help you determine whether the investment is worth.
